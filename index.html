Why this matters

business impact
  • Leadership mistakes are expensive. A misaligned general manager can stall growth, disrupt culture, and require a second search.
  • Delayed leadership hires slow expansion. Open critical roles delay projects, hiring, and revenue.
  • Candidate market reputation compounds. Repeated restarts and inconsistent communication reduce response rates over time.
  • Retention directly affects cost. Clear role definition, structured interviews, and onboarding plans improve first year retention and reduce replacement hiring.

The problem

what is breaking today
  • CEO burden. Leaders are pulled into coordination and admin rather than decision points.
  • Inconsistent representation. Outside recruiters lack context and the story changes by search.
  • Misaligned incentives. Placement is optimized instead of quality and retention.
  • Pipeline stall. Outreach slows once a decent candidate appears and searches restart.
  • Repeat work. Role definitions and interview plans are recreated at every company.

Role description

responsibilities and scope

Primary responsibilities

  • Define roles before launch. Outcomes, must haves, compensation band, interview plan.
  • Run priority searches. Sourcing, screening, orchestration, offer strategy and close.
  • Align interviewers. Scorecards and calibration.
  • Represent Northrim and each company. Accurate and consistent messaging.
  • Maintain a leadership bench. Ongoing outreach and relationship building.

Ongoing operating work

  • Interviewer training. Structured interviewing and evaluation.
  • Onboarding plans. 30, 60 and 90 day expectations.
  • Recruiter enablement. Coach local recruiters where present.
  • Retention reviews. Post hire feedback loops.
  • Portfolio playbooks. Reusable templates and processes.

What this reduces

business impact
  • Agency fees. Typical retained searches cost 20 to 30 percent of first year salary. A $150,000 hire often carries $30,000 to $45,000 in fees.
  • CEO coordination time. Coordinating candidates, emails, scheduling, and follow up can consume 5 to 10 hours per week during a search. At an estimated executive value of $200 to $300 per hour, that is $1,000 to $3,000 per week per search.
  • Failed searches. Restarting a search doubles outreach time and candidate fatigue and can add 6 to 12 weeks of vacancy.
  • Vacancy delay. Open leadership roles slow hiring, projects, and revenue. Even a conservative $10,000 per week impact over 8 weeks equals $80,000 in delayed output.
  • Retention and replacement cost. Each replacement typically repeats recruiting expense ($20,000 to $30,000), onboarding and training time, productivity ramp loss, and management rework. Improving retention reduces repeated recruiting and disruption rather than relying on salary multiples.
  • Key person dependency. Shared playbooks and benches reduce repeated setup across companies.

Across multiple companies and multiple hires per year, small improvements in speed, retention, and agency usage compound into meaningful portfolio level value.

Compounding portfolio capability

why internal ownership matters
  • CEO time protection compounds. If each leadership search absorbs about 64 CEO hours, across 13 hires that is roughly 832 hours annually redirected to operations and growth.
  • Shared learning. What is learned from one hire improves the next role definition, interview plan, and close across all companies.
  • Faster future searches. A maintained leadership bench reduces time to slate and lowers restart risk.
  • Acquisition readiness. A repeatable hiring playbook accelerates leadership placement in newly acquired companies.
An internal owner turns hiring from a recurring project into an operating capability that improves every year.
